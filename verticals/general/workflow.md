# General Proposal Workflow

This workflow is used for proposals that do not fall into the Data Center / Mission
Critical, Life Science / Pharmaceutical, or Food & Beverage / CPG verticals.

---

## Process Overview

1. Administrative Setup & RFP/RFQ Receipt
2. Review Specifications and Requirements
3. Develop Scope of Work for Subcontractors (if applicable)
4. Subcontractor Procurement & Bid Management (if applicable)
5. Get Quotes from Suppliers / Material Estimation
6. Build Estimate
7. Create Proposal
8. Review (Peer Review, Management Review)
9. Submit Proposal

---

## Step 1: Administrative Setup & RFP/RFQ Receipt

- Receive and organize the RFP/RFQ package
- Create project folder and document repository
- Assign pursuit team
- Conduct bid/no-bid evaluation
- Define win theme and proposal strategy

## Step 2: Review Specifications and Requirements

- Review all provided drawings, specifications, and scope documents
- Perform material takeoff and equipment counts
- Identify scope boundaries and ownership (company vs. client/OFCI vs. subcontractor)
- Submit RFIs for any ambiguous or missing information
- Document assumptions for incomplete information

## Step 3: Develop Scope of Work for Subcontractors

- Draft SOW for each subcontractor trade as needed
- Review SOW with operations team before distribution
- Distribute for subcontractor bids

## Step 4: Subcontractor Procurement & Bid Management

- Source and qualify subcontractors
- Send RFQs and bid packages
- Conduct sub meetings and provide support
- Receive, vet, and level subcontractor proposals
- Select subcontractors

## Step 5: Get Quotes from Suppliers / Material Estimation

- Send RFQs to equipment and material vendors
- Obtain panel shop quotes (internal or external)
- Estimate expenses (travel, per diem, equipment, permits, etc.)

## Step 6: Build Estimate

- Input all cost data into the estimate workbook
- Estimate labor hours by role category
- Create project schedule
- Estimate travel and per diem costs
- Peer review the completed estimate

## Step 7: Create Proposal

- Assemble proposal using the appropriate template
- Draft cover letter and executive summary
- Write project scope sections
- Compile clarifications, assumptions, and exclusions
- Transfer pricing from estimate workbook
- Identify supplemental deliverables required by the RFP

## Step 8: Review

- Peer review of proposal document
- Management review and approval
- Margin review with executive team
- Incorporate feedback and finalize

## Step 9: Submit Proposal

- Final QA check of all deliverables
- Submit via the customer's specified channel
- Archive final submission package

---

## AI Agent Notes

The general workflow follows the same foundational structure as the vertical-
specific workflows but without industry-specific compliance requirements,
specialized terminology, or vertical-specific RACI assignments. The agent
should adapt tone and technical depth based on the RFP content.
